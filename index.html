Standard Tibetan is a subset of the Central Ü dialect group, also known as Lhasa Tibetan. Although it does not encompass the technical entirety of Tibetic language speakers (as can be seen above), it is the most widely spoken Tibetic language and the official language of both the Tibetan Autonomous Region and the Tibetan government-in-exile. Therefore, for the purposes of this analysis, it will function as the "Tibetan language."

Tournadre estimates that almost six million people, worldwide, speak Tibetic languages, although he notes that "this figure is approximate since there is no precise and reliable census" (Tournadre, 2014, p. 12). His own estimate only accounts for fifty or so of the many Tibetic languages, of which he suggests there are nearly 200 seperate varieties, each of varying relation and susbsequent mutual intelligibility.
